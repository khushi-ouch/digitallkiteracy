Social Media Checklist for College Students

 Do’s
- Think before posting anything online
- Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication
- Respect others’ opinions and communicate politely
- Verify information before sharing it
- Maintain privacy settings on your accounts

 Don’ts

- Don’t engage in cyberbullying or online arguments
- Don’t spread fake news or unverified content
- Don’t use offensive or inappropriate language
- Don’t accept friend requests from unknown people

Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India. It involves tricking users into transferring money through fake payment requests or scams.

A typical case happens step-by-step like this: First, the fraudster contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or social media, pretending to be a bank official, buyer, or customer support agent. Next, they create urgency or trust, such as offering a refund or asking for verification. Then, they send a UPI “collect request” or QR code and convince the victim that accepting it will *receive* money. In reality, the victim ends up authorizing a payment. Sometimes, they also ask for OTPs or PINs, which leads to direct account access.

Students, online shoppers, and people unfamiliar with digital payments are the most common targets. Fraudsters often exploit lack of awareness and quick decision-making.

The consequences can be severe, including financial loss, stress, and difficulty in recovering money. In many cases, victims realize the fraud too late.

This type of crime highlights the importance of digital literacy and cautious behavior while using online payment platforms.

 Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

 General Safety Tips
- Never share your OTP, PIN, or passwords with anyone
- Always verify unknown links or messages before clicking
- Use strong and unique passwords for different accounts
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions
- Keep your apps and devices updated regularly

 UPI / Financial Safety Tips
- Never approve a “collect request” unless you are paying someone
- Remember: scanning a QR code or approving a request sends money, not receives it
- Double-check the receiver’s name before confirming payment
- Do not trust calls claiming to be from bank officials asking for details

 Reporting Cybercrime
- Report incidents at: https://cybercrime.gov.in
- Call Helpline: 1930 (India Cyber Crime Helpline)
